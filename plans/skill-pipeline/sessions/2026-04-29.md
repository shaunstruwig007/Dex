## What we learned (skill-shape evidence)

The manual walkthrough was a test harness for the skills. Concrete evidence by skill:

### `/initiative-discovery-custom` (was Moneypenny)

- **Confirm-relevance per grep hit** is a real behaviour, not theatre. The walkthrough hit a stale "future phase" reference in `Scheduled_Content.md` that misled discovery for ~1 hour. The new skill makes pruning explicit.
- **Evidence-gap detection** is load-bearing when the vault is sparse. Today's vault was sparse (empty Companies/, sparse Meetings/, no ICP). Without an explicit "here's what I couldn't find" log, discovery silently fabricates conviction.
- **Stakeholder extraction** (named SMEs the PM should talk to) was useful — Jan came up repeatedly as the historical multi-lang owner. The skill captures this as a structured field, not a passing mention.
- The runtime/schema-anchored `discovery.*` field complexity from the old moneypenny skill (field envelope, source flavours, atomic writes) was over-engineered for a markdown-native skill. Stripped.

### `/prd-author-custom` (was Bond)

- **Required input contract** is the right move. Without discovery, "drafting a PRD" is just dressed-up opinion. The walkthrough's discovery phase made the PRD draft 10× sharper.
- **Idempotence rule** — author edits are signal. Silently overwriting them is the easiest way for the skill to lose trust.
- **Refusal list** caught real things during the manual draft: an aspirational metric (engagement parity without baseline), a risk without mitigation, a slice 1 candidate that wasn't actually a walking skeleton.
- **`plan-mode-seed` fence** is the load-bearing contract. The 1:1 mapping to Cursor Plan mode is what makes the skill earn its keep over `/agent-prd`.

### `/critique-product-custom` + `/critique-engineering-custom` (was M + Q)

- Both critique skills earned their keep on a real PRD. Both surfaced real things: M caught the "slice 1 demos to engineering not customer" framing problem; Q caught the missing `lang` attribute on translated content (screen-reader correctness).
- The 7 rows (M) and 9 rows (Q) all held up. No row was redundant. No row was obviously missing.
- **Pair protocol (M → Q)** was correct. M locked the why, Q sharpened the how. Reverse order would have produced cheaper alternatives that weren't yet tied to the right outcome.
- **`eng-alt` mandatory** — Q's "cheaper path" row was the highest-yield row across both critiques. Three alternatives offered, all real options. Made it a hard rule in the new skill.

### `/weekly-market-intel-custom` (was Felix)

- The user explicitly named this as standalone, not pipeline-coupled. Validated.
- The existing skill was the most operationally mature of the five — minimal change needed beyond the rename + downstream reference repointing.

### What did NOT need a skill

- **Design-as-its-own-step** — the multi-language feature was light on design, so a separate `/design-prompt-custom` skill produced no incremental value over a "Design pointers" section in the PRD itself. **Decision: design-prompt skill killed**, design pointers folded into `/prd-author-custom`. Worth re-validating on a design-heavy use case (next walkthrough).
- **PDLC card-state runtime concerns** — the old moneypenny skill was tightly coupled to the parked pdlc-ui runtime (atomic writes, skill_run events, cost ceilings). Removed entirely; the new skill is markdown-native.
