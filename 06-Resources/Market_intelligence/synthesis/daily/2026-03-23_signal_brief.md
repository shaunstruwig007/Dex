## Contrarian & novel angles

### Contrarian (pushback or alternative read)

- **AI hiring lawsuit ≠ broad AI backlash:** The Workday case is specifically about automated *screening/rejection* (legally exposed under disparate impact law). AI in *communications, safety, engagement* sits in a different risk category — [[Wyzetalk]]'s AI assistant is advisory, not decisional. Don't let the lawsuit narrative dampen the AI-in-EX story.
- **SA capital concentration:** Most of the March SA raises ($6.1M) are fraud/payments/fintech infra — not workforce tech. The ecosystem is maturing but not directly funding deskless EX competitors yet. Orca Fraud and Optasia are B2B infra plays, not proxies for [[Wyzetalk]] comp set.
- **Frontline onboarding as IC story:** Ragan frames it as communications, not HR ops. Buyers reading Ragan are IC leads, not CHRO/ops buyers — useful for positioning language, less so for direct pipeline signal.

### Novel outcomes (second-order, non-obvious)

- **Compliance moat for deskless:** If AI hiring tools face regulatory heat, procurement teams will scrutinise *all* AI-in-HR tools more carefully. [[Wyzetalk]]'s approach — transparent comms, not automated decisions — could become a **compliance differentiator** in RFPs where competitors position agent-driven workflows.
- **ChatGPT job discovery (ZipRecruiter):** If job discovery moves into chat interfaces, frontline worker attention moves further away from traditional portals. Platforms already embedded in daily frontline comms (like [[Wyzetalk]]) could become the natural channel for internal mobility and shift offers — **internal talent marketplace adjacency**.
- **Optasia's JSE scale story:** 432M users / 38 countries at ~$0.61 ARPU shows African tech can scale at consumer-internet volumes with financial-grade reliability. Sets a reference point for what "scale" looks like in the African enterprise context — useful for investor and board narratives.
