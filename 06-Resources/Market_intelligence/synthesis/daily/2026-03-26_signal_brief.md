## Contrarian & novel angles

*Use this section to stress-test the obvious narrative — what smart skeptics might believe, and non-obvious second-order effects.*

### Contrarian (pushback or alternative read)

- **Humand is LatAm-first, not Africa-first.** MINISO/Domino's/OXXO are LATAM retailers. Wyzetalk's regulated industries (mining, energy, SA/Africa) aren't Humand's current hunting ground. The $66M is a signal to *watch*, not panic over — yet.
- **"70% of interactions moving to conversational" is vendor-produced data.** WhatsApp HR adoption stats in the wild skew toward SMB India/LATAM use cases. Enterprise regulated-sector (mining, SA banking) may lag 2–3 years. Don't over-index on the stat in a boardroom deck.
- **Category consolidation cuts both ways.** Enterprise buying fewer, thicker platforms also means buyers are increasingly *not starting* with specialist deskless tools — they extend their HRIS. Wyzetalk's value prop must survive the "can't Workday/SAP just do this?" objection with hard operational data.

### Novel outcomes (second-order, non-obvious)

- If Humand continues scaling in retail/food globally, they may land in SA and approach **Wyzetalk's existing clients** (quick service restaurants, retail chains) before Wyzetalk's Essential tier is priced/packaged. **Sequence matters: ship a tier that's defensible before they arrive.**
- **AI-as-trust framing** in regulated industries could flip the "AI is scary" objection into a sales asset — if Wyzetalk shows that its AI features are auditable + HR-governed, it positions better than consumer-grade AI chat slapped onto a comms platform.
- **Zoom × Workvivo bundling** may push some customers to *two platforms* for desk and frontline — creating an explicit budget for the deskless layer that didn't previously exist. Position for that budget line, not against Zoom.
