## How to read the loss reasons

1. **HubSpot status** (`Hubspot Status: Deal lost reason`) is the structured pick list — good for **volume**.
2. **Deal Lost Reason (Description)** + **Main Points** carry **why** in sales language — use for **narrative** (pricing, stack, competitor, ghosting).
3. **Competitor Presence** is **inconsistent** (sometimes “Internal solution”, sometimes a product name) — treat as a **signal**, not a full competitive census.

## What this says about *why* we lose (plain English)

### 1. Ghosting & stalled engagement
- **HubSpot** often labels these **Ghosted** or **Lost/Ghosted**; free text repeats **no response**, **postponed**, **backburner**.
- **Implication:** pipeline hygiene, clearer **next-step commitments**, and **multi-threading** beyond a single sponsor — not only a product gap.

### 2. Price & budget
- Explicit **Too expensive**, **lack of budget**, or **cost** language appears across descriptions — sometimes tied to **ROI proof** or **contract structure** concerns.
- **Implication:** packaging, **land-and-expand**, and **TCO narrative** (especially for large headcount / frontline-heavy accounts).

### 3. Incumbent & “good enough” internal stack
- **Meta Workplace**, **Teams**, **SharePoint**, **internal comms** — buyers default to **global mandates** or **existing licences**.
- **Implication:** **integration**, **identity**, and **frontline-specific value** (reach, ops, safety, payslips) must be sharper than “another app.”

### 4. Competitors & substitutes
- Named products appear (e.g. **JEM**, **Staffbase**, **Workplace**) plus **generic** “lost to another supplier”.
- **Implication:** keep **competitor pages** and **WhatsApp / deskless** parity on the roadmap visible in discovery (see Essential launch context).

### 5. Technical fit & requirements
- **Technical requirements**, **integrations**, **SSO** — deals die when **IT / security** bar isn’t met.
- **Implication:** **security & compliance** story (incl. payslip / external app flows) belongs **early** in evaluation, not only in procurement.

### 6. Deskless mechanics: payslips, USSD, data-light
- Recurring language: **payslips**, **USSD**, **data-light**, **notice boards** — aligns with **frontline** positioning.
- **Implication:** roadmap items (**federated app / payslips**, **WhatsApp**, **messaging**) are **deal-relevant**, not nice-to-haves.
