## Problem formulation

- **Who has the problem, when, and what is broken today**  
  - **Mining / heavy-industry operators and community-relations teams** need to push **time-sensitive, location-specific, and reputational** information to **people who are not on the payroll** (nearby residents, local authorities, applicants, contractors’ families, etc.) — e.g. blast windows, safety notices, recruitment drives.  
  - **Today’s Wyzetalk-shaped product surface in the vault** is framed around **employees**: audience primitives ([`06-Resources/PRDs/Current/Groups.md`](../../PRDs/Current/Groups.md)) are **imported employee attributes** and **admin-managed groups**; messaging and urgent alerts ([`06-Resources/PRDs/Current/Messaging_Ops_Urgent_Alerts.md`](../../PRDs/Current/Messaging_Ops_Urgent_Alerts.md)) target **Everyone / saved groups / directory** inside the tenant — not a general “external public.”  
  - **Gap:** A **community app** implies **identity, consent, segmentation, and moderation** models that differ from deskless employee comms; without a deliberate product, customers likely rely on **SMS blasts, Facebook/WhatsApp informal groups, town halls, or paper** — high friction, weak audit trails, or inconsistent reach.

- **Success looks like…**  
  - Organisations **publish** curated updates to **registered community members** with predictable delivery (push/SMS/WhatsApp per policy), **geography or topic** relevance, and **evidence** of send/reach where required for safety or compliance.  
  - Clear **separation** from employee Blue/Essential: no accidental mixing of blast notifications meant for neighbours with internal urgent alerts.

## Solution directions

| Option | Summary | Trade-offs |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **A — Separate “Community” product / tenant type** | Distinct app listing, registration, admin console for **external audiences**; optional linkage to employer brand only. | **Pros:** Clean trust boundary, independent roadmap, simpler compliance story. **Cons:** Duplicate build of feed/notification basics unless shared platform services. |
| **B — Remote app / micro-frontend under Air** | Use [`Template_Remote_App.md`](../../PRDs/Next/Template_Remote_App.md) pattern: **public or light-auth** remote app + APIs for publishes. | **Pros:** Matches existing federated strategy; faster iteration per tenant segment. **Cons:** Still need **core APIs** for messaging, identity, and analytics. |
| **C — Extend Groups + messaging to “external segments”** | New group type for **non-employee** contacts imported or self-registered. | **Pros:** Reuses [`Groups.md`](../../PRDs/Current/Groups.md) and messaging primitives. **Cons:** High **data-model and abuse risk**; contradicts current **employee-only** group definition — likely the heaviest core change. |
| **D — Partner-led (e.g. WhatsApp / SMS-only community channel)** | One-way templates to opted-in community numbers; minimal UI. | **Pros:** Fast; aligns with WhatsApp investment. **Cons:** Weak **owned** community experience; Meta/policy dependency ([`WhatsApp_Channel.md`](../../PRDs/Current/WhatsApp_Channel.md)). |

**Recommendation (discovery-level):** Favour **A or B** — treat external community as a **deliberately bounded** product with **public/light-auth** onboarding ([`Template_Remote_App.md`](../../PRDs/Next/Template_Remote_App.md)), and **reuse** delivery channels (SMS, push, WhatsApp) from [`Communication.md`](../../PRDs/Current/Communication.md) with **new consent and template** rules — rather than stretching **employee Groups** ([`Groups.md`](../../PRDs/Current/Groups.md)) to non-employees without a full design pass.

## Novel ideas

- **Blast + “quiet hours” geo-fence:** Pair **scheduled blast notifications** with **map-based opt-in zones** (mine blast radius vs township) — different from employee **urgent alert takeover** ([`Messaging_Ops_Urgent_Alerts.md`](../../PRDs/Current/Messaging_Ops_Urgent_Alerts.md)), which is in-app mandatory for workers.  
- **Dual ledger:** Same incident → **internal urgent** (employees) + **external advisory** (community) with **linked but distinct** copy and audit — reduces reputational errors.  
- **Jobs funnel as first wedge:** “New job applications” could be a **thin** community module (careers feed + apply CTA) that proves registration and engagement before full blast/safety modules.  
- **CSR / social licence analytics:** Align with mining trend content on **community** and **cultural competency** ([`tracking-the-trends-2025.md`](../../Research/Industry_research_reports/summaries/Mining%20and%20Metels/tracking-the-trends-2025.md)) — position as **licence to operate** instrumentation, not only comms.

## Risks and assumptions

- **Assumption:** A subset of existing customers (mining, energy, municipalities) will **pay** for stakeholder comms distinct from employee EX — not validated in vault PRDs.  
- **Risk:** **Misuse of urgent/blast patterns** — confusion with **employee urgent alerts** ([`Messaging_Ops_Urgent_Alerts.md`](../../PRDs/Current/Messaging_Ops_Urgent_Alerts.md)) could create **alert fatigue** or **wrong audience** if not isolated.  
- **Risk:** **Privacy / consent** for non-employees (POPIA/GDPR/local mining-community norms) — stricter than employee notification preferences ([`Communication.md`](../../PRDs/Current/Communication.md)).  
- **Risk:** **Moderation and liability** for user-generated content if the app ever becomes two-way — parallels [`Feed_social_layer.md`](../../PRDs/Future/Feed_social_layer.md) compliance concerns, amplified for publics.  
- **Assumption:** Technical **public auth** path is acceptable to security for **read-mostly** community content — needs explicit review vs [`Elevated_Auth_Remote_App.md`](../../PRDs/Next/Elevated_Auth_Remote_App.md) employee flows.
