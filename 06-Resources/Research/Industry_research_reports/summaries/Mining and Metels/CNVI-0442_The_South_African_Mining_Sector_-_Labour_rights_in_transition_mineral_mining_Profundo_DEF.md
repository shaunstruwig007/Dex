Summary 
The working conditions for mine workers in South Africa are perilous, leading to numerous 
fatalities annually despite legal protections for Freedom of Association. Mining companies often 
hinder unionisation efforts through practices like contract labour and outsourcing, contributing to 
the persistent impoverishment of workers despite incremental wage improvements. The urgency 
to address these challenges is critical, particularly given South Africa's significant role in the global 
production of transition minerals. As CNV Internationaal explores the South African mining sector, 
it aims to develop informed strategies to engage with upstream and downstream stakeholders, 
advocating for improved conditions throughout the mineral supply chain. 
This report presents the results of a study that mapped the mineral mining value chain in South 
Africa, identifying key stakeholders such as producers, traders, processors, and buyers.
