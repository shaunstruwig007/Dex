Deal volume bounces back with continued interest from overseas buyers
After a steady decline in deal volume in the 
previous three quarters, Q3 has bounced back 
with an impressive 27 deals matching only Q4 
2023 and marking the highest quarterly number 
recorded on our 5-year chart. Of these 27 deals, 
44% involved an overseas acquiror, an increase 
from 38% last quarter. This demonstrates a 
continued healthy appetite from international 
buyers to invest in UK assets. Deal value is 
heavily skewed in Q3 by the £2.7bn acquisition 
of Evri by Apollo Funds. 
Notable cross border consolidation included the 
French Stef Group, growing its UK presence with 
the acquisition of Long Lane Deliveries through 
its UK subsidiary Langdons. This is part of STEF 
Group’s growth strategy as a European pure 
player in the food supply chain. The Danish Danx 
Group continued with its buy and build strategy 
and acquired Logik Logistics International Ltd, 
expanding the Group’s time-critical logistics 
capabilities and strengthening its on-demand/ 
ad-hoc service platform. 
Q3 also confirmed an agreement from the 
Supervisory board of Deutsche Bahn AG to sell 
its logistics subsidiary to Danish DSV Group for 
a reported EUR 14.3bn EV. Subject to agreement, 
the transaction is expected to complete in 2025.
Trade deals accounted for 74% of transactions, 
2% down on last quarter. It highlights 
continued interest from equity investors 
(26%) in the sector. If we include PE-backed 
companies to this statistic we reach 48%, 
almost half of the transactions recorded being 
PE or PE-backed buyers. 
Tech-enabled logistics businesses have continued 
to attract trade buyers such as Aptean Inc 
and Kerridge Commercial Systems and equity 
investors such as BGF and Mercia, and we saw 
a significant rise in tech-enabled transactions 
from 33% last quarter to 41% in Q3. 
The BDO Barclays 2024 Logistics Confidence 
Index report published in September showed 
a bounce back in overall sector confidence 
reaching a score of 57 .6 up from 47 .3 last year. 
This increase in confidence appears to be based on 
the expectation of a more stable and manageable 
trading environment and the resilience built up 
across the industry having navigated and operated 
essential supply chains every day in consistently 
demanding economic conditions. 
Interestingly, M&A activity appears to be less of 
a priority for the coming year with only 34% of 
survey respondents saying they are likely to make 
an acquisition over the next 12 months, compared 
to 40% in 2023. This expectation for M&A is at its 
lowest level since 2019 which reflects a number 
of factors: high interest rates, trading challenges 
and a relatively short supply of appropriate quality 
assets at an acceptable price.

Performance
The BDO International Logistics Index reported a marginal incline this 
quarter, with DSV being the biggest climber delivering a 27% quarterly 
value increase. However, this reflects a need to recoup value as on 
a three year rolling period DSV has declined by 23%. US based CH 
Robinson and Chinese SF Holding also had strong quarters realising 
strong gains. Meanwhile South African SuperGroup, French Bpost, 
Nordic DFDS and US Fedex all suffered quarterly high teen declines. 
In assessing the three-year trend, the BDO Europe index has performed 
considerably poorer in comparison to the US. The strongest performers 
on a 3 year rolling period were ID Logistics Group SA and  Stef SA who 
have seen their market capitalisation rise significantly by 52% and 36% 
respectively. Whilst in the US there was a positive performance from 
GXO Logistics delivering 36% growth in the three year period, influenced 
by their recent acquisition of Wincanton and the announcement of them 
exploring a potential sale.
