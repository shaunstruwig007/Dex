# Humand

## Snapshot

- **Category:** Deskless worker OS — comms + HR self-service + operations + learning in one mobile app
- **Focus:** Deskless / frontline (purpose-built; no meaningful desk/knowledge-worker play)
- **Founded:** ~2020 (Argentine founders)
- **HQ:** Buenos Aires; expanding US + Brazil
- **Funding:** $66M Series A (Feb 2026, Kaszek Ventures + Goodwater Capital) — largest HR Tech Series A in Latin America. Angels: Arash Ferdowsi (Dropbox), Guillermo Rauch (Vercel), Marcos Galperin (MercadoLibre)
- **Scale:** 1.6M+ workers, 1,500+ organisations, 51 countries
- **Customers:** Siemens, Home Depot, John Deere, Domino's, Chili's, MINISO, OXXO, ArcelorMittal, Lumen Technologies

## Product

Single mobile app combining:

- **Comms:** Social feed (Instagram-style), chat, digital magazine, live streaming, news broadcast
- **HR self-service:** Payslips, time-off, onboarding, digital employee files, benefits
- **Operations:** Time tracking, task management, forms, service tickets
- **Talent:** Performance reviews, OKRs, learning modules, surveys, recognition (kudos)
- **AI layer:** Native agents for onboarding guidance, policy Q&A, request routing — not bolt-on

No login / low-friction access for workers without corporate email.

## Vs **Wyzetalk**

- **Overlap:** Mobile-first frontline platform; comms + HR self-service; deskless ICP; WhatsApp/messaging adjacency; retail, manufacturing, food & beverage verticals
- **Differentiation (their edge):** Significantly broader HR feature depth (performance, learning, OKRs natively); well-funded; stronger LatAm brand; notable Fortune 100 logos
- **Differentiation (our edge):** Africa/SA market presence and regulatory depth; regulated-industry positioning (mining, energy, financial services); WhatsApp-native channel (not just mobile app); coexistence with Teams/Workplace; **Wyzetalk** Essential pricing likely more accessible mid-market; auditable/AI-governed story for regulated buyers
- **Geography:** Humand is LATAM-first, North America-expanding. SA/Africa is not their current hunting ground — **watch, not panic, for now**
- **Risk vector:** If Humand expands into SA retail/QSR, they could approach existing **Wyzetalk** clients (quick service, retail chains) before Essential tier is fully packaged. Sequence matters
